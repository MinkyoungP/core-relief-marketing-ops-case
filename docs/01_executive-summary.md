## 1. Project Background

CoreRelief is a Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) brand that relies heavily on paid marketing channels for customer acquisition. 
As marketing spend increases, it becomes important not only to track revenue, but also to understand contribution margin and acquisition efficiency.

This project was designed to evaluate marketing budget allocation from a Marketing Operations perspective, focusing on contribution margin, customer segment performance, and budget reallocation scenarios.

## 2. Business Problem

The main business question of this project is:

> Can reallocating marketing budget across customer segments improve contribution margin without significantly hurting total revenue?

Some customer segments generate high revenue but lower margins, while others generate lower revenue but higher margins. 
This creates a trade-off between revenue growth and profitability.

This project analyzes this trade-off and simulates different budget allocation scenarios.

## 3. Analysis Approach

The analysis was conducted in the following steps:

1. Understand the business model and revenue structure
2. Analyze revenue and contribution margin by customer segment
3. Identify high-revenue vs high-margin segments
4. Simulate budget reallocation scenarios
5. Estimate the impact of CPA increases on contribution margin
6. Compare baseline vs reallocation scenario

SQL was used to structure and analyze the dataset, and scenario simulations were conducted to evaluate potential business outcomes.

## 4. Key Findings

- High-revenue segments were not always the most profitable segments.
- Shifting budget toward higher-margin segments improved the contribution margin structure.
- However, increasing CPA reduced the expected revenue gain from budget reallocation.
- The simulation showed that budget reallocation alone does not guarantee profit improvement if acquisition costs increase.

  ## 5. Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were made:

- Budget allocation should consider contribution margin, not just revenue.
- High-margin segments should be prioritized, but CPA changes must be monitored.
- Scenario-based planning should be used before major budget reallocation decisions.
- Marketing and Finance teams should align on contribution-based performance metrics.
  
  ## 6. Limitations & Assumptions

This project is based on a simulated dataset and several assumptions:

- Revenue mix changes were simulated.
- CPA increases were estimated for certain segments.
- Fixed costs were excluded from the contribution margin calculation.

Therefore, the results should be interpreted as a directional business case, not as actual financial projections.
